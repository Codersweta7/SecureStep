Cybersecurity Tips

1. Password Security

  • Use strong passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g., P@ssw0rd!123).
  • Avoid reusing the same password across multiple accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible for an extra layer of protection.
  • Store your passwords securely using trusted password managers instead of writing them down.

2. Recognizing Phishing Scams

  • Be cautious of emails or messages asking for personal information, especially if they seem urgent.
  • Check the sender's email address and look for signs like typos or unofficial domains.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.
  • When in doubt, contact the company directly through their official website or phone number.

3. Safe Browsing Practices

  • Only visit websites that begin with https:// and show a padlock icon in the address bar.
  • Avoid entering sensitive information on public Wi-Fi without using a VPN.
  • Clear your browser cookies and cache regularly to maintain privacy.
  • Be mindful of pop-ups and ads, especially those that seem “too good to be true.”

4. Protecting Your Devices

  • Keep your devices updated with the latest software and security patches.
  • Install and regularly update a trusted antivirus or anti-malware program.
  • Avoid downloading apps or software from unofficial or unknown sources.
  • Lock your devices with PINs, patterns, or biometric authentication like fingerprints.

5. Understanding Social Media Safety

  • Be cautious about what personal information you share on social platforms.
  • Adjust privacy settings to control who can see your posts and profile details.
  • Avoid accepting friend requests or messages from strangers.
  • Regularly review and remove permissions for third-party apps linked to your accounts.

6. Backups and Data Recovery

  • Regularly back up your important files to an external drive or a secure cloud service.
  • Check that your backup is encrypted to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Test your backup by restoring files occasionally to ensure it works as expected.
  • Know how to recover your accounts if you lose access, such as using recovery options or security questions.
